Practice – Self-Compassion Break

Developed by Dr. Kristin Neff

The Self-Compassion Break is a brief practice that incorporates all three elements of self-compassion. It can be used in any moment of difficulty, from minor stress to intense suffering.

1

Mindfulness

Acknowledge that you're experiencing suffering. Place your hand on your heart if you wish, and feel the warmth and gentle touch of your hand.

"This is a moment of suffering."

2

Common Humanity

Remind yourself that suffering is part of life and something we all experience. You are not alone in your struggle.

"Suffering is part of life. Many others feel this way."

3

Self-Kindness

Offer yourself kindness and support. Ask yourself: "What do I need to hear right now to express kindness to myself?"

"May I be kind to myself in this moment."

Physical Gestures

Adding a physical gesture can enhance the practice:

  • Place a hand over your heart
  • Hold your own hand
  • Place both hands on your belly
  • Gently touch your cheek

Helpful Tips

To make this practice more effective:

  • Personalize the phrases to feel authentic
  • Start with small difficulties before big ones
  • Practice regularly, not just in crisis
  • Be patient with yourself as you learn
