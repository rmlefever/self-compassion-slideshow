Activity – The Compassionate Letter

Writing a compassionate letter to yourself is a powerful way to practice self-compassion. This exercise helps you access your inner compassionate voice.

Writing Instructions

  1. 1

    Think of something you're struggling with or feeling self-critical about.

  2. 2

    Imagine a kind, wise, compassionate friend who knows you well, including your strengths and weaknesses.

  3. 3

    Write a letter to yourself from the perspective of this compassionate friend.

  4. 4

    After writing, put the letter aside. Return to it later and allow yourself to receive the compassion.

Helpful Sentence Starters

"I understand that you're feeling... because..."

"It makes sense that you would feel this way because..."

"You're not alone in this. Many people experience..."

"You're doing your best, and I appreciate how you..."

"One thing you might consider is..."

Helpful Tips

  • Write in the second person (using "you") to create distance from self-criticism.

  • Include understanding, validation, and encouragement in your letter.

  • Be specific about the situation you're addressing.

  • Remember that this letter is just for you—no one else needs to see it.

  • If writing is difficult, you can draw, speak aloud, or record your compassionate message.
