Three Elements of Self-Compassion

According to Dr. Kristin Neff's research, self-compassion consists of three interconnected components that work together to create a compassionate mindset toward ourselves.

Mindfulness

Recognizing and acknowledging our suffering without exaggerating or suppressing our pain.

"This is a moment of suffering."

Common Humanity

Recognizing that imperfection is part of the shared human experience, not something that happens to "me" alone.

"Suffering is part of life."

Self-Kindness

Being warm and understanding toward ourselves when we suffer, fail, or feel inadequate, rather than ignoring our pain or being self-critical.

"May I be kind to myself."

"With self-compassion, we give ourselves the same kindness and care we'd give to a good friend."
