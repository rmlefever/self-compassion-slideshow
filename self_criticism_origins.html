Where Self-Criticism Comes From

Self-criticism develops through various life experiences and serves as a misguided attempt to protect ourselves. Understanding its origins can help us approach it with more compassion.

Childhood Experiences

Often internalized from critical parents, caregivers, or authority figures. We absorb their voices and continue their criticism.

Trauma Response

Can develop as a way to make sense of painful experiences. "If I'm at fault, I can control it next time" becomes a safety strategy.

Social & Cultural Pressures

Unrealistic standards from media, social comparison, and cultural expectations create impossible ideals that fuel self-judgment.

Protective Function

Self-criticism often develops as a misguided attempt to motivate ourselves or protect us from external criticism or rejection.
