Common Humanity – We're All in This Together

Common humanity is recognizing that suffering is part of the shared human experience, not something that happens to "me" alone.

What is Common Humanity?

The understanding that imperfection, failure, and suffering are universal experiences that connect us to others rather than separate us.

Connection: Recognizing our shared humanity helps us feel less isolated in our struggles.

Perspective: Seeing our experiences as part of being human helps reduce shame and self-judgment.

"Suffering is part of life."

Common Humanity vs. Isolation

Common Humanity Isolation
"We all struggle sometimes" "I'm the only one who fails"
Connects us to others Separates us from others
Reduces shame Increases shame
Opens us to support Leads to hiding and withdrawal

Practice Tip

When you're struggling, try asking yourself: "How many other people might be experiencing something similar right now?" This simple question can help shift from isolation to connection.
