What is Self-Criticism?

Self-criticism is the harsh, judgmental inner voice that focuses on our perceived flaws, mistakes, and inadequacies.

Internal Bully: Often sounds like a harsh critic or judge that constantly evaluates and finds you lacking.

Echoes from the Past: May repeat messages from childhood, school experiences, or traumatic events.

Misguided Protection: Often intended to motivate or protect us, but actually increases shame and suffering.

Impact on Recovery

Self-criticism makes recovery from mental health challenges, addiction, and trauma more difficult by:

  • Increasing feelings of shame and worthlessness
  • Depleting emotional resources needed for healing
  • Reinforcing negative beliefs about oneself
  • Creating resistance to self-care and compassion
