Mindfulness – Recognizing Suffering

Mindfulness is the first component of self-compassion. It involves noticing and acknowledging our pain without exaggerating or suppressing it.

What is Mindfulness?

A non-judgmental awareness of the present moment, including our thoughts, feelings, and bodily sensations, without trying to change or fix them.

Awareness: We must first notice our suffering before we can respond with compassion.

Balance: Mindfulness helps us avoid both suppressing our feelings and becoming overwhelmed by them.

"This is a moment of suffering."

Mindfulness vs. Over-identification

Mindfulness Over-identification
Observing thoughts and feelings Being consumed by thoughts and feelings
Balanced awareness Exaggerated reactions
"I feel sad" "I AM sadness"
Creates space for response Triggers automatic reactions

Practice Tip

When you notice difficult emotions arising, try naming them: "I notice anxiety is present" rather than "I'm anxious." This creates a small but powerful space between you and the emotion.
