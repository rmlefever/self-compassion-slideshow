Self-Kindness – Being a Friend to Yourself

Self-kindness is treating yourself with the same warmth, care, and understanding that you would offer to a good friend who is struggling.

What is Self-Kindness?

Actively comforting yourself, offering understanding, and responding to your own pain with care rather than harsh criticism.

Active Support: Self-kindness is an active practice of offering yourself comfort, encouragement, and understanding.

Growth-Oriented: Self-kindness supports positive change through encouragement rather than criticism.

"May I be kind to myself in this moment."

Self-Kindness vs. Self-Judgment

Self-Kindness Self-Judgment
"I'm doing my best" "I should be doing better"
Warm, supportive tone Harsh, critical tone
Motivates through encouragement Motivates through fear
Builds resilience Depletes emotional resources

Practice Tip

When you notice self-criticism, ask yourself: "What would I say to a friend in this situation?" Then offer those same kind words to yourself.
