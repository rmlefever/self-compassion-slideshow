Building a Daily Practice

Self-compassion is like a muscle that grows stronger with regular practice. Here are simple ways to incorporate self-compassion into your daily life.

Mindful Check-In

Set a reminder to pause briefly throughout the day. Notice how you're feeling without judgment, and offer yourself a moment of kindness.

Try setting 3 alarms on your phone as reminders.

Self-Compassion Break

Practice the 3-step self-compassion break whenever you notice you're struggling. Just 30 seconds can shift your perspective.

Keep the 3 phrases written on a small card or as a phone wallpaper.

Kind Self-Talk

Notice your inner dialogue. When you catch self-criticism, pause and ask: "How would I speak to a friend in this situation?"

Practice rephrasing critical thoughts with kindness.

Bedtime Reflection

Before sleep, reflect on moments of difficulty during your day and offer yourself compassion for those challenges.

Place a hand on your heart as you do this reflection.

Common Humanity Reminder

When feeling isolated in your struggles, remind yourself: "Many others feel this way too. I'm not alone in this experience."

Notice others around you who might be having similar feelings.

Compassion Journal

Keep a journal where you write to yourself with kindness about difficult experiences, celebrating small wins and acknowledging challenges.

Even just 5 minutes of writing can be beneficial.

Remember

Self-compassion is a practice, not perfection. Be patient with yourself as you develop this skill. Even small moments of kindness toward yourself make a difference.
