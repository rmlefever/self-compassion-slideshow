What is Self-Compassion?

Self-compassion is treating yourself with the same kindness, concern, and support you'd show to a good friend.

Honest & Kind: Self-compassion acknowledges difficulties and responds with kindness rather than harsh judgment.

Strength-Based: It's about courage to face our pain and take responsibility with kindness, not avoiding accountability.

Scientifically Supported: Research shows self-compassion improves mental health, resilience, and motivation.

What Self-Compassion Is & Isn't

Self-Compassion IS Self-Compassion IS NOT
Taking responsibility with kindness Making excuses for yourself
Motivating yourself with encouragement Being complacent or lazy
Acknowledging shared humanity Feeling sorry for yourself (self-pity)
Balanced awareness of emotions Exaggerating or suppressing feelings
Valuing yourself unconditionally Valuing yourself based on achievements
